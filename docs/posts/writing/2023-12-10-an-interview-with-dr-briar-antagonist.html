As a reader of fiction and lover of all good stories, one of the things I find most troubling is movies, books, novels, and stories that have spent an inordinate amount of time building a protagonist with a realistic feel, believable back-story and motivations, only to have him face either a faceless force as an antagonist or a â€˜bad-guyâ€™ that comes across as one-note, plastic, and/or shallow.

As I explored my Protagonist, and added a second, exploring both, I found that their journey was only half the puzzle. 

If I am going to understand the antagonistic force my protagonists (Ash, Dezi) face, I must understand his (Dr. Briar, antagonist) as well. I must spend just as much time considering his character arch as I do theirs. Except in this case, his character arch will be a negative change arch. We will find his life working well for him at the start, and unraveling as he squeezes his hands tighter around the Want/Lie, rejecting his Need/Truth.

While the following interview will probably never make it into the book in whole, and many details will change, especially where he begins to sound too over-the-top, I think this was a successful practice interview with my fictional character.
