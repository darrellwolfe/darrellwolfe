Wisdom from the text of Acts 2:41-47

Fellowship koinonia was the defining characteristic of the Way. Koinonia was exemplified by a community that gathered regularly to listen to those who had been with Yeshua teach about the promises regarding Yeshua in the Tanakh, and through the stories and parables of Yeshua about the way the Kingdom of God community is to be lived out. The Way followed the ancient Jewish customs of regular rhythm of prayers and built their community around shared meals and shared resources. While not an endorsement of government socialism, this community took care of the poor among them so that nobody had any needs. Sometimes, this was facilitated by a wealthier member selling property or belongings to give to those who had less. Prayers (formal and informal) created an atmosphere that was centered on Yahweh, with Yeshua as head of the assembly, through the leading of the Holy Spirit.
